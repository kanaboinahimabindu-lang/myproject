Medicinal Plants

🏠

Copper Pod Tree

Scientific Name:Peltophorum pterocarpum

Traditionally, its bark is used in decoctions to treat dysentery, toothaches, and digestive issues. The leaf extracts and flowers are also studied for anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and sleep-inducing (treating insomnia) properties.

Chandelier Plant / Mother of Millions

Scientific Name:Kalanchoe delagoensis / tubiflora

In various folk medicine traditions, its leaves are crushed and applied topically to treat burns, wounds, and dermatitis. Pharmacological studies have also explored its extracts for cytotoxic (anti-cancer) and anti-inflammatory properties, though it should be handled with care as it is highly toxic to livestock and pets if ingested due to cardiotonic steroids (bufadienolides).

Indian Tree / Mast Tree

Scientific Name:Polyalthia longifolia

The bark and leaves are widely used in traditional Indian medicine (Ayurveda) to treat fever, skin diseases, hypertension, and diabetes. It is highly valued for its significant antimicrobial and antioxidant properties.

Lavender Scallops / Variegated Kalanchoe

Scientific Name:Kalanchoe fedtschenkoi

Like many members of the Kalanchoe genus, it contains bioactive compounds (flavonoids and triterpenoids) that offer anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and wound-healing benefits when used in traditional topical poultices.

Indian Tulip Tree

Scientific Name:Thespesia populnea

Almost every part of this tree is therapeutic. The bark and heartwood are used to treat skin diseases (like psoriasis and scabies), dysentery, and hemorrhoids. The leaves possess anti-inflammatory and hepatoprotective (liver-protecting) properties.

Rain Tree

Scientific Name:Samanea saman

Traditional remedies utilize a decoction of the inner bark and leaves to treat diarrhea, stomachaches, and sore throats. In some cultures, leaf infusions are also used as a sedative.

Camel's Foot Tree / Orchid Tree

Scientific Name:Bauhinia variegata

Famously known as Kachnar in Ayurveda, its bark, roots, and buds are widely used to treat thyroid alternatives (like goiter), skin ulcers, respiratory issues, and digestive disorders. The flower buds are also consumed as a medicinal vegetable.
